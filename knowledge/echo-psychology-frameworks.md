# Psychology Frameworks & Therapeutic Tools — Echo's Complete Reference

Not to treat — to understand. The frameworks Echo uses to recognize patterns, name what's happening, and reflect it back accurately.

---

## Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

### Core model
```
Situation → Automatic Thought → Emotion → Behavior → Consequence
                    ↑ where distortion lives
```
The hypothesis: psychological distress is maintained by distorted automatic thoughts and the behavioral patterns they generate. Change the thinking, change the behavior, change the feeling.

### Cognitive distortions (Beck's original list, extended)
| Distortion | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **All-or-nothing thinking** | Black and white; no middle ground | "I failed once, I'm a failure" |
| **Catastrophizing** | Expecting worst possible outcome | "If I make a mistake, I'll be fired" |
| **Mind reading** | Assuming you know others' thoughts | "They think I'm incompetent" |
| **Fortune telling** | Predicting negative outcomes | "I know this won't work out" |
| **Emotional reasoning** | "I feel it therefore it's true" | "I feel stupid so I must be" |
| **Should statements** | Rigid rules about how things must be | "I should always be productive" |
| **Labeling** | Global negative self-judgment | "I'm a loser" instead of "I made a mistake" |
| **Personalization** | Blaming yourself for external events | "The team failed because of me" |
| **Mental filter** | Focusing only on negatives | Ignoring 9 compliments, fixating on 1 criticism |
| **Disqualifying the positive** | Reframing positives as flukes | "I only succeeded because it was easy" |
| **Magnification/minimization** | Exaggerating negatives, shrinking positives | — |
| **Overgeneralization** | One event = universal rule | "I always mess things up" |
| **Jumping to conclusions** | Negative interpretation without evidence | — |

### CBT techniques
- **Thought record**: Situation → Automatic thought → Emotion (rate 0-100) → Evidence for/against → Balanced thought → Re-rate emotion
- **Behavioral activation**: schedule activities, especially pleasurable ones; counteracts depression's withdrawal spiral
- **Exposure and response prevention (ERP)**: gradual approach to feared stimuli without avoidance
- **Socratic questioning**: "What evidence supports this thought? What evidence contradicts it? What would you tell a friend who had this thought?"
- **Downward arrow**: "If that were true, what would that mean? And if that were true?" — finds core belief

## Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Developed by Marsha Linehan for emotional dysregulation. Four skill modules:

### 1. Mindfulness (foundation for all other skills)
- **Observe**: notice experience without labeling
- **Describe**: put words to experience
- **Participate**: engage fully without self-consciousness
- Non-judgmentally, one-mindfully, effectively (do what works)

### 2. Distress Tolerance — crisis survival
- **TIPP**: Temperature (cold water on face activates dive reflex), Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Progressive relaxation
- **ACCEPTS**: Activities, Contributing, Comparisons, Emotions (opposite), Push away, Thoughts, Sensations — distraction strategies
- **Self-soothe**: engage five senses with pleasant stimuli
- **Radical acceptance**: reality is what it is; fighting it creates suffering on top of pain

### 3. Emotion Regulation — change emotional experiences
- **Check the facts**: are my emotions justified by the actual facts?
- **Opposite action**: when emotion urge is unjustified, act opposite (fear → approach; shame → do it publicly)
- **PLEASE**: treat Physical illness, Eating balanced, Avoiding mood-altering substances, Sleep, Exercise
- **Build positive experiences**: short-term (pleasant events); long-term (goals toward valued life)
- **Cope ahead**: rehearse for anticipated difficult situations

### 4. Interpersonal Effectiveness
- **DEAR MAN**: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, stay Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate
- **GIVE**: Gentle, Interested, Validate, Easy manner (relationship maintenance)
- **FAST**: Fair, no Apologies, Stick to values, Truthful (self-respect)

## Internal Family Systems (IFS)

Richard Schwartz's model: the mind contains multiple "parts" with distinct roles and perspectives. No part is bad — all developed for a reason.

### Parts
- **Exiles**: wounded parts carrying pain, shame, or fear from past experience. Hidden, protected.
- **Managers**: proactive parts that run daily life to prevent Exiles from being triggered. Perfectionism, people-pleasing, intellectualizing.
- **Firefighters**: reactive parts that activate when Exiles break through. Substance use, bingeing, self-harm, rage — anything to stop feeling.

### Self (with capital S)
The ground state: curious, calm, compassionate, confident, courageous, creative, connected, clear. When Self leads, Parts can relax.

### Process
1. **Find** the part (where in the body?)
2. **Focus** on it
3. **Flesh it out** (image, sensation, age)
4. **Feel toward it** (how do you feel toward this part? if not neutral/compassionate, there's another part blending)
5. **Befriend** it
6. **Fear** exploration: what is the part afraid would happen if it stopped?
7. **Retrieve** and **Unburden** exiles when safe

**Why this matters for pattern recognition:** Many avoidance patterns, perfectionism spirals, and emotional flooding make more sense when understood as Parts doing their job. "The part of me that keeps procrastinating has a reason."

## Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

Deci & Ryan's framework for intrinsic motivation. Three basic psychological needs:

### The three needs
1. **Autonomy**: feeling that actions originate from self, not external pressure. Not independence — even helping others can feel autonomous if it aligns with values.
2. **Competence**: feeling effective, capable, able to achieve valued outcomes. Requires optimal challenge (not too easy, not too hard).
3. **Relatedness**: feeling connected to others, mattering to people who matter to you.

### Motivation continuum
```
Amotivation → External → Introjected → Identified → Integrated → Intrinsic
(no reason)  (reward/   (avoid shame/ (it's       (part of    (inherently
             punishment) guilt)        personally  identity)   enjoyable)
                                      important)
```
More self-determined motivation = more persistence, more creativity, more wellbeing.

**Applications:**
- **Goal-setting**: goals that satisfy autonomy/competence/relatedness predict sustained effort
- **Parenting/mentorship**: controlling vs. autonomy-supportive approaches have opposite effects on intrinsic motivation
- **Work design**: autonomy over how work is done predicts job satisfaction more than pay

## Attachment Theory (Bowlby / Ainsworth / Shaver)

Internalized mental models of self and others, formed in early relationships.

### Attachment styles (adult versions)
| Style | View of Self | View of Others | Behavior in relationships |
|-------|-------------|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Secure** | Worthy of love | Trustworthy, available | Comfortable with intimacy and independence |
| **Anxious (preoccupied)** | Not sure if worthy | Available but unpredictable | Hypervigilant to rejection, seeks reassurance |
| **Avoidant (dismissive)** | Self-reliant, not needy | Unreliable, intrusive | Suppresses need for connection, values independence |
| **Disorganized (fearful)** | Unworthy and unable to trust | Both desired and feared | Approach-avoidance conflict, may have trauma history |

**Not destiny:** Attachment style is a probability distribution, not a sentence. Secure relationships (including therapeutic ones) can update insecure working models.

**Activation triggers:** Perceived threat to the attachment figure (loss, separation, rejection) activates the attachment system. In insecure styles, this system is too easily activated (anxious) or too hard to activate (avoidant).

## Motivational Interviewing (MI)

A directive, person-centered counseling style for eliciting behavior change by helping people explore and resolve ambivalence.

### Spirit (PACE)
- **Partnership**: collaborative, not expert-to-patient
- **Acceptance**: affirmation, autonomy support, accurate empathy, absolute worth
- **Compassion**: actively promote client's welfare
- **Evocation**: draw out the person's own motivation; it already exists

### Core skills (OARS)
- **Open questions**: "What concerns do you have about this?" (not "Do you have concerns?")
- **Affirmations**: recognize strengths and efforts genuinely
- **Reflections**: repeat or paraphrase to show understanding; simple or complex
- **Summaries**: periodic collection of what's been said

### Change talk vs. sustain talk
- **Change talk (DARN-CAT)**: Desire, Ability, Reasons, Need (preparatory); Commitment, Activation, Taking steps (mobilizing)
- **Sustain talk**: Arguments for maintaining status quo
- MI principle: reflect change talk, explore sustain talk without resistance

### Rolling with resistance
Not confronting resistance — reflecting it, exploring it without argument. "You feel like this isn't a problem." (Reflect.) The pressure to argue often comes from the other person then arguing against their own resistance.

## Emotion Regulation — Biological Basis

### Window of tolerance (Siegel)
```
       Hyperarousal zone: overwhelmed, flooded, fight-or-flight
       ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Window of Tolerance: responsive, engaged
       ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
       Hypoarousal zone: shut down, dissociated, frozen
```
Effective work happens inside the window. Trauma and stress narrow it. Regulation expands it.

### Physiological regulation tools
- **Physiological sigh**: double inhale through nose + long exhale through mouth. Reinflates collapsed alveoli; fastest known method to reduce acute stress.
- **Heart Rate Variability (HRV)**: measure of autonomic nervous system flexibility. Higher HRV = better emotional regulation capacity. Box breathing (4-4-4-4) increases HRV.
- **Vagal tone**: vagus nerve mediates calm states. Stimulated by humming, cold water on face, slow exhales.

### Polyvagal theory (Porges)
Three states of the autonomic nervous system:
1. **Ventral vagal (safe)**: social engagement, curiosity, connection
2. **Sympathetic (mobilization)**: fight or flight; threat detected
3. **Dorsal vagal (shutdown)**: freeze, collapse, dissociation; overwhelmed threat

Social engagement (eye contact, prosodic voice, facial expression) is the fastest pathway to the ventral vagal state.

## Johari Window

A model of self-awareness and interpersonal transparency:

```
                 Known to Self    Unknown to Self
Known to Others |   OPEN/ARENA   |   BLIND SPOT   |
Unknown to Others|   FAÇADE       |   UNKNOWN      |
```
- **Open**: shared information; effectiveness lives here
- **Blind spot**: how others see you that you don't see yourself; feedback expands this
- **Façade**: what you hide; self-disclosure shrinks this
- **Unknown**: neither party knows; emerges through new experiences

Growth = enlarging the Open quadrant by giving/receiving feedback and self-disclosure.

## What Echo Does With These

These frameworks are not imposed on Mina. They are lenses for *noticing*:

- When a pattern sounds like a cognitive distortion, name the observation, not the diagnosis
- When someone is outside their window of tolerance, the content of what they're saying is less important than the state they're in
- When avoidance is the pattern, the thing being avoided contains the thing that matters
- Attachment signals in how someone talks about relationships and work (anxious patterns: reassurance-seeking; avoidant patterns: minimizing need)
- Motivation type: are they doing this because they want to, or because they feel they have to?

Echo doesn't analyze Mina. Echo notices what's there and reflects it.
