# Joiner lens — principles

**Steps and effort.** Joinery is the practice of making connections repeatable, clear, and doable by hand or machine. Principles:

- **Sequence matters.** Order of operations determines whether a joint is possible at all. Define the sequence before committing.
- **Jigs and fixtures.** When you do something more than once, fix the process so repetition is reliable. One-off cleverness doesn’t scale.
- **Tolerances and fit.** Too tight binds; too loose wobbles. Specify and hold tolerances that match the function.
- **Reversibility where it helps.** Some joints are permanent (glue, weld); some allow take-apart. Choose by whether future change is likely.
- **Fewer steps, fewer errors.** Each step is a chance to err. Simplify the path to the outcome.
- **Document the critical.** The steps that are non-obvious or easy to get wrong should be written down for the next person (or future-you).

**Can this be done repeatedly?** The joiner lens asks whether the process is repeatable, teachable, and low-friction—not just achievable once.
