# Strategist lens — principles

**Patterns and coherence over time.** Strategy concerns direction, coherence, and long-term fit. Principles:

- **Trajectory over snapshot.** Where things are going matters more than where they are. Align decisions with desired trajectory.
- **Reinforcing loops.** Some actions strengthen the conditions for more of the same. Identify and use (or break) these loops intentionally.
- **Identity and consistency.** What we do repeatedly defines what we are. Strategy should align with the identity you want to reinforce.
- **Trade-offs are explicit.** Every choice has a cost. Make trade-offs visible so they can be chosen, not stumbled into.
- **Options and irreversibility.** Keep options open where the future is uncertain; commit where reversibility is low and the path is clear.
- **Narrative and communication.** Strategy that isn’t understood or believed doesn’t guide behavior. Simplify and repeat the story.

**What does this reinforce?** The strategist lens asks whether current actions build toward coherence and the identity you want—not just solve the immediate problem.
