# Geometry lens — principles

**Alignment and proportion.** Geometry deals with shape, relation, and invariance. Principles that apply to systems and plans:

- **Reference frames.** Position and direction only make sense relative to a frame. Establish a clear reference (origin, axes, units) and keep it consistent.
- **Invariants.** Some properties stay fixed under change (angles, ratios, topology). Identify invariants so you know what must hold.
- **Composition and decomposition.** Complex shapes are built from simpler ones. Decompose to understand; compose to build.
- **Scale and proportion.** Relationships that hold at one scale can break at another. Check that reasoning and constraints scale with the problem.
- **Alignment and drift.** Off-by-one and small misalignments compound. Define “aligned” and check it at boundaries and over time.
- **Constraints and degrees of freedom.** Every constraint removes a degree of freedom. Over-constrain and nothing fits; under-constrain and behavior is undefined.

**Does this stay on trajectory?** The geometry lens asks whether structure, alignment, and proportion are correct and stable—not just locally consistent.
