# Product lens — principles

**Value and exchange.** Product thinking focuses on outcomes, use, and sustainable value. Principles:

- **Outcome over output.** Delivering a feature is not the goal; the goal is the change in user or business outcome. Measure and optimize for outcomes.
- **Who pays, who benefits.** Value flows between users, customers, and the organization. Align incentives so that creating value is rewarded.
- **Cost of delay.** Doing something later has a cost: lost learning, missed opportunity, or compounding debt. Prioritize by cost of delay, not only by effort.
- **Minimum viable and learning.** The smallest change that tests an assumption is often better than the full solution. Learn fast, then invest.
- **Sustainability of value.** One-off wins that can’t be repeated or maintained don’t scale. Prefer systems that generate ongoing value.
- **Energy and payoff.** Effort is finite. Allocate it where the return is highest and where it reduces future effort.

**Is this worth the energy?** The product lens asks whether the work creates real, sustainable value for the right parties—not just activity.
